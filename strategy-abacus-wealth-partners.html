How Values-Driven Investing Works: Aligning Your Portfolio with What Matters

Why this idea

Intro · fully scripted 00:00 – 01:20

The hook and the promise

"If you just went through an IPO, the most expensive decision of your life is not which stock to buy. It is what you do in the next ninety days." Most tech operators think the hard part is over once the company goes public. The truth is the opposite. The windfall arrives faster than the plan, and the two biggest threats to it, concentration and taxes, move quietly while everyone is celebrating. I have sat across from operators who watched two million dollars evaporate, not from a bad investment, but from a handful of avoidable decisions made in the wrong order. In the next few minutes I am going to walk you through the three mistakes I see most often, and the simple framework we use to avoid all of them. Stay to the end and I will show you exactly where to start.

Point 01 · outlined 01:20 – 04:30

The mistake we see most often instead of a schedule

  • Why the post-IPO adrenaline leads to reactive selling and bad timing.
  • The case for a pre-set, tax-aware selling schedule decided before liquidity.
  • Quick story: an operator who set a 10b5-1 plan and kept his outcome boring, in the best way.
Point 02 · outlined 04:30 – 07:45

Mistake two: holding a concentrated position too long

  • The psychology of loyalty to the company stock, and why it quietly compounds risk.
  • How to think about diversification without triggering an unnecessary tax bill.
  • A simple way to size down a position over time on your terms.
Point 03 · outlined 07:45 – 10:30

Mistake three: meeting an advisor after the event, not before

  • Why the most valuable planning happens before the liquidity event, not after.
  • What a real pre-liquidity plan looks like, shown on screen as a framework.
  • The cost of waiting, made concrete with a side-by-side comparison.
Outro & CTA · outlined 10:30 – 12:00

The calm invitation

  • Recap the three mistakes in one tight sentence each.
  • Point to the free lead magnet in the description: the Equity Exit Playbook.
  • One low-pressure CTA to book a 15-minute call if they want a second set of eyes.
